Poetry

Trolley Tracks

A poem I wrote about being a commuter student at a prestigious university, realizing that my "fun" public transit adventures were built on foundations of POC exclusion and education deserts.

unboxing, unpacking

A poem I wrote upon realizing my family hadn't put up a Christmas tree in years and childhood memories weren't forever.

it takes a village

A poem I wrote about the Filipino practice of Bayanihan, where neighbors support each other by helping them relocate, referencing the environmental disasters climate change has brought to the islands.

Inheritance

A poem using three forms: the golden shovel, erasure poetry, and the hay(na)ku structure. Rooted in my last visit to the Philippines, this piece highlights government corruption and youth activists struggling to unite old and new generations.

Bikini

An etymological poem about how we unknowingly preserve harmful language, referencing the Bikini Atoll nuclear site, its cancerous impact on Marshallese communities, and how it birthed the term, "bikini".

Bunso

A mixed-media piece incorporating family letters and photos, discussing the heavy expectations, both good and bad, of the "Bunso" title given to the youngest of a family. Forever dedicated to my Lola and Lolo.
